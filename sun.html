SUN- Ball of Fire

Our Sun is a 4.5 billion-year-old yellow dwarf star, a hot glowing ball of hydrogen and helium at the center of our solar system. It's about 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from Earth and it's our solar system's only star. Without the Sun's energy, life as we know it could not exist on our home planet. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.
